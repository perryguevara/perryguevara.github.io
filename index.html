Perry Guevara, PhD

Environmental, Health, & Digital Humanist

Perry joined the Dominican faculty in 2016. He teaches in a number of areas including Literature and the Humanities, Global Public Health, Service Learning, and the Master of Arts in Humanities program. He also directs the undergraduate minor in Performing Arts & Social Change, which features a distinctive curricular partnership with Marin Shakespeare Companyâ€™s theater-prison program. His courses cover topics such as medieval and early modern literature, philosophy of science, science writing for public health, Shakespeare through the lens of animal studies, ancient literature of the Mediterranean, effective communication through podcasting, and the formal study of poetry and fiction. He has authored essays on affect theory and Shakespearean pedagogy in prison, embodied cognition and entomologic life in Titus Andronicus, and transitional object theory in Othello. Beyond teaching and research, Dr. Guevara serves on the Honors Board.
